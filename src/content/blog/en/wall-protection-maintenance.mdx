A well-maintained wall protection system can last 20 to 30 years. Poorly maintained, it may require replacement after only 5 to 7 years. Here are the essential protocols every facility manager should integrate into their preventive maintenance plan.

## Recommended Inspection Frequencies

| Facility Type | Visual Inspection | Full Inspection |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Hospitals and LTC facilities | Weekly | Semi-annual |
| Office buildings | Monthly | Annual |
| Shopping centers | Weekly | Semi-annual |
| Residential | Quarterly | Every 2 years |

## Cleaning Protocols by Material

### Stainless Steel

- **Routine cleaning**: warm water + pH-neutral soap, wipe in the direction of the grain
- **Disinfection**: 1% sodium hypochlorite solution (rinsing required)
- **Stubborn stains**: baking soda paste, no aggressive abrasives
- **Avoid**: concentrated bleach (attacks passive layer), steel wool, chlorine products without rinsing

### Anodized Aluminum

- **Routine cleaning**: soapy water, soft sponge
- **Anodized layer care**: aluminum polish once per year
- **Avoid**: concentrated alkaline products (caustic soda), strong acids, abrasives

### Polymers (Nylon, HDPE)

- **Cleaning**: all common disinfectants, including hydrogen peroxide and quaternary ammonium
- **UV disinfection** possible as a supplement for critical areas
- **Fastener inspection**: check for cracks at fixing points

### Wood

- **Cleaning**: damp cloth, never immersed
- **Maintenance**: oiling (teak or linseed oil) every 6-12 months for bare wood
- **Spot treatment**: fine sanding (220 grit) and re-oiling for scratched areas
- **Warning signs**: swelling, blackening, or cracking → immediate intervention required

## Critical Inspection Points

### Wall Fasteners

Screws, anchors, and fixings are the most common failure points. At each inspection:

1. Visually check for rust or corrosion
2. Test handrail rigidity (no abnormal play)
3. Inspect the joint between the handrail and mounting plate (no gap, no standing water)
4. Check wall integrity around fixings (no cracking)

### Joints and Connections

Assemblies are entry points for moisture and dirt:

- Check joint sealing (fill with sealant if needed)
- Verify alignment of end-to-end elements
- Inspect condition of end caps and terminations

### Surface Finishes

- Scratches on stainless: directional sanding with 400-grit, finish with Scotch-Brite
- Anodizing flaking: apply clear lacquer to stop progression
- Deep scratches on polymer: replace the affected element

## Preventive Maintenance Plan: Annual Template

### January — Winter Inspection

- Check exterior handrails after first frosts
- Verify absence of cracking from freeze-thaw cycles
- Test all fasteners

### April — Spring Cleaning

- Full cleaning of all elements (interior and exterior)
- Apply maintenance products (polish, oil, etc.)
- Condition report with photographs

### July — Summer Inspection

- Check UV-exposed zones (discoloration)
- Inspect drainage systems around exterior installations

### October — Winter Preparation

- Anticorrosion treatment on exterior metal parts
- Preventive tightening of all fasteners
- Complete annual report to submit to the property owner

## When Should a System Be Replaced?

Preventive replacement criteria (before failure) include:

- Active corrosion on more than 10% of the surface
- Mechanical play exceeding 5 mm at the free end
- Material cracking or delamination
- Non-compliance with current standards (height, diameter)
- Irreparable aesthetic damage in a representative space
