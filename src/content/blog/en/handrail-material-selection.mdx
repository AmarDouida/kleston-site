Choosing the right handrail material commits your installation for 15 to 25 years. A poor choice — aluminum in a marine environment, wood in a high-traffic corridor — translates into high maintenance costs and premature replacements. Here is our complete guide to making the right decision from the start.

## Selection Criteria

Before comparing materials, clearly define your usage context:

1. **Interior or exterior**?
2. **Traffic volume**: dozens or thousands of passes per day?
3. **Chemical environment**: humidity, salt, aggressive cleaning products?
4. **Aesthetic constraints**: desired finish, architectural integration?
5. **Total budget** (purchase + installation + maintenance over 10 years)

## Stainless Steel: The Durable Standard

### Available Types

- **304 (18/8)**: general interior and sheltered exterior use
- **316L**: marine environments, pools, food industries

### Advantages

- Exceptional corrosion resistance (316L)
- High mechanical strength (ultimate load > 500 MPa)
- Hygienic, easy to disinfect
- Timeless aesthetic, available in multiple finishes (brushed, mirror-polished, satin)
- Service life: 25-40 years without replacement

### Disadvantages

- Higher purchase price (×1.5 to ×2 vs aluminum)
- Thermally conductive: cold to the touch in winter
- Greater weight: requires reinforced wall anchors

### Ideal For

Pools, commercial kitchens, high-traffic public spaces, premium buildings

---

## Anodized Aluminum: The Versatile Option

### Advantages

- Lightweight (density 2.7 vs 7.9 for steel)
- Good corrosion resistance in non-marine environments
- Wide range of anodizing colors
- Easy to cut and adjust on site
- Excellent value for money

### Disadvantages

- Less mechanically resistant than steel
- Sensitive to chlorinated environments (pools) — avoid without special treatment
- Anodizing may chip over time in high-grip zones

### Ideal For

Offices, retail, residential building corridors, interior stairways

---

## Nylon and Technical Polymers: The Healthcare Choice

### Available Types

- **Polyamide 6 (PA6)**: standard use
- **High-density polyethylene (HDPE)**: increased chemical resistance
- **Antimicrobial compounds**: integration of bacteriostatic agents

### Advantages

- Non-conductive: warm to the touch
- Resistant to hospital cleaning and disinfection products
- Can integrate antimicrobial agents (ISO 22196 standard)
- Ergonomic profiles available (ovalized, bilobed)
- Easy to cut on site

### Disadvantages

- Plastic appearance, less prestigious visually
- Lower mechanical strength (sufficient for regulatory loads)
- UV-sensitive (color fading outdoors)

### Ideal For

Hospitals, long-term care facilities, clinics, schools

---

## Wood: Warmth and Authenticity

### Recommended Species

- **Oak**: hard, resistant, good durability
- **Beech**: fine grain, excellent for intensive use
- **Bamboo**: renewable, very hard, trending

### Advantages

- Unique aesthetic warmth
- Renewable and recyclable
- Can be stained in any shade

### Disadvantages

- Regular maintenance required (annual oiling or varnishing)
- Sensitive to moisture: discouraged outdoors without special protection
- Prone to scratches and impacts
- Price varies by species

### Ideal For

Hotels, luxury residences, heritage buildings, spaces with a wood identity
