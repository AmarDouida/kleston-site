Universal accessibility is a right in Quebec. The **Act to secure handicapped persons in the exercise of their rights with a view to achieving social, school, and workplace integration** requires public bodies to plan and implement accessibility measures. For private buildings open to the public, the Quebec Construction Code (CCQ) sets minimum standards.

## Who Is Affected by Accessibility Obligations?

Accessibility obligations apply to any building classified as **Group A** (assembly), **B** (care or detention), **D** (business and personal services), or **E** (retail), whenever it is open to the public.

For existing buildings, renovation work affecting more than 50% of the building's value triggers the obligation to comply with accessibility standards.

## Requirements for Adapted Handrails

### Dimensions and Gripping

Accessibility handrails must meet precise dimensions:

- **Diameter**: between 32 mm and 50 mm for optimal grip
- **Clearance**: minimum 45 mm between the handrail and any adjacent surface (wall, partition)
- **Profile**: circular or ovalized with rounded edges (radius ≥ 6 mm)

### Horizontal Extensions

At the ends of stairways and ramps, the handrail must extend **horizontally 300 mm** beyond the nosing of the first and last step. This extension allows users to stabilize themselves before beginning or completing their descent.

### Continuity and Interruptions

The handrail must be **continuous** along the entire length of the stairway or ramp. The only permitted interruptions are those required by intermediate landings, where the handrail may be interrupted by a maximum of 100 mm.

## Visual Contrast: A Commonly Overlooked Requirement

The Code requires that handrails be visually distinct from their background. The luminosity ratio between the handrail color and the wall must be at least **70%**.

In practice, this means:
- A white handrail on a white wall is non-compliant
- A gray handrail on a gray wall is often non-compliant
- A natural wood handrail on a beige wall may be non-compliant depending on the shades

## Access Ramps: Technical Specifications

For accessibility ramps, requirements are even more stringent:

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Maximum slope | 1:12 (8.33%) |
| Minimum clear width | 920 mm |
| Maximum length without landing | 9,000 mm |
| Guardrail height | 1,070 mm minimum |
| Wheel stop | 75 mm minimum height |

## Tactile Signage and Braille

For spaces serving visually impaired individuals, handrails can integrate **tactile indicators**:
- Change in texture 300 mm from the start and end of the ramp
- Floor number in relief and Braille at intermediate landings

These elements are not mandatory in all buildings but are strongly recommended for public-facing establishments.

## Accessibility Compliance Process

### Step 1: Accessibility Audit

A comprehensive audit identifies all non-conformities: handrail height, missing horizontal extensions, insufficient clearance, inadequate visual contrast.

### Step 2: Compliance Plan

The plan prioritizes interventions based on their accessibility impact and establishes a timeline compatible with the building's operations.

### Step 3: Certified Installation

## Available Financial Assistance

The **Société d'habitation du Québec (SHQ)** and certain municipalities offer subsidy programs for accessibility work. These programs can cover 50% to 90% of eligible costs for owners of collective residential buildings.

## Conclusion

Universal accessibility is both a legal obligation and a competitive advantage for commercial buildings. Compliant installations reduce liability risks and open your establishment to a broader clientele.
